Computer Colour Models
Computer colour models define standards used to represent, store, display, and manipulate colours in computers.
Two primary colour models.
RGB
RGB is an additive model, where a colour is generated by combining the light intensity of the three colours red, green, and blue.
RGB is used in digital screens (monitors, TVs, cameras).
Using RGB
CMYK
CMYK is a subtractive model, where a colour is generated by absorbing the light intensity of the three colours cyan, magenta, and yellow.
CMYK is used in printing.
